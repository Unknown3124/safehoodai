Emergency Procedures

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

For adults, children, and infants who are unresponsive and not breathing normally

Difficulty: Moderate Time: Continuous until help arrives
1

Check for Responsiveness

Tap the person's shoulder and shout "Are you okay?" Look for any movement or response.

Tip: If no response, immediately call 108 or have someone else call.
2

Call Emergency Services

Dial 108 immediately. If someone else is present, have them call while you begin CPR.

Important: Every second counts in cardiac arrest.
3

Position the Person

Place the person on their back on a firm, flat surface. Remove any clothing from the chest area.

Safety: Ensure the scene is safe before approaching.
4

Locate Compression Point

Find the center of the chest (between the nipples). Place the heel of one hand on this spot.

Position: Keep your arms straight and shoulders directly above your hands.
5

Begin Chest Compressions

Push hard and fast at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute. Allow the chest to recoil completely between compressions.

Depth: Compress at least 2 inches (5 cm) for adults.
6

Give Rescue Breaths (Optional)

After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if trained. If not trained, continue compressions only.

Note: Hands-only CPR is effective for bystanders.
7

Continue Cycles

Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths (or compressions only) until help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

Signs of Life: Breathing, movement, or response to touch.
8

Use AED if Available

If an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is available, use it as soon as possible. Follow the device's voice prompts.

Safety: Continue CPR until the AED is ready to analyze.

Heimlich Maneuver (Abdominal Thrusts)

For conscious adults and children over 1 year who are choking and cannot speak, cough, or breathe

Difficulty: Easy Time: Until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious
1

Assess the Situation

Ask "Are you choking?" If the person nods yes but cannot speak, they need the Heimlich maneuver.

Signs: Universal choking sign (hands around throat), inability to speak or cough.
2

Position Yourself

Stand behind the person. Wrap your arms around their waist. Make sure they are standing or sitting upright.

Stability: Keep your feet shoulder-width apart for balance.
3

Locate the Compression Point

Find the navel. Place your fist just above the navel, in the center of the abdomen.

Position: Thumb side of fist against the abdomen.
4

Perform Abdominal Thrusts

Grasp your fist with your other hand. Give quick, upward thrusts into the abdomen.

Technique: Thrust inward and upward as if trying to lift the person.
5

Continue Until Effective

Repeat thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

Warning: If person becomes unconscious, begin CPR immediately.

First Aid Basics

Essential first aid procedures for common injuries and emergency situations

Difficulty: Easy Time: Varies by situation
1

Ensure Scene Safety

Before approaching any emergency, make sure the scene is safe for you and others.

Check for: Traffic, fire, electrical hazards, toxic substances.
2

Assess the Person

Check if the person is conscious and breathing. Look for signs of life and responsiveness.

ABCs: Airway, Breathing, Circulation.
3

Control Bleeding

Apply direct pressure to wounds with a clean cloth or bandage. Elevate the injured area if possible.

Pressure: Maintain pressure for at least 10-15 minutes.
4

Treat for Shock

Keep the person warm and comfortable. Elevate their legs if no spinal injury is suspected.

Signs: Pale skin, rapid breathing, weak pulse, confusion.
5

Call for Help

Call emergency services for serious injuries, persistent bleeding, or if the person's condition worsens.

When to call: Unconsciousness, severe bleeding, chest pain, difficulty breathing.
